üîç The Volume Flip: Already Done

On Feb 10, 2026, Artemis published data showing Hyperliquid processed $2.6 trillion in notional trading volume in 2025 ‚Äî nearly double Coinbase's $1.4 trillion.

This was independently confirmed by crypto.news, BeInCrypto, MoneyCheck, and DailyCoin.

Important nuance: Hyperliquid's volume is primarily perpetual futures with leverage. Coinbase is mostly spot. Perps naturally generate higher notional volume. But even with that caveat ‚Äî a fully on-chain DEX doing 2x the volume of the largest US exchange is remarkable. The demand for on-chain derivatives is clearly there.

‚ö° Why It Could Happen (Coinbase Flip)

1. The Flywheel Is Real

Hyperliquid's Assistance Fund converts ~97% of trading fees into HYPE buybacks. With $29B in peak daily volume generating ~$6M in daily fees, that's serious buy pressure. More volume ‚Üí more fees ‚Üí more buybacks ‚Üí higher price ‚Üí more attention ‚Üí more volume.

2. Supply Squeeze

The team cut their own unlock allocation by 90% ‚Äî from 1.2M to 140,000 HYPE/month. That's a strong signal. When a team voluntarily reduces their own token distribution, they're betting on future price appreciation.

3. Product Expansion Is Aggressive

HIP-3 opened permissionless commodity markets (gold, silver, FX). HIP-4 adds prediction markets on testnet. They're not staying in the perps lane ‚Äî they're going after TradFi products on-chain.

4. Coinbase Listing Unlocks Institutional Flow

HYPE/USD went live on Coinbase on Feb 5. The irony of Coinbase listing its own competitor is not lost on anyone. This gives US institutional traders easy access to HYPE exposure.

5. Coinbase Is Weakening

COIN stock is down ~27% YTD while HYPE is up ~31.7%. The market is already repricing the relative value of centralized vs decentralized exchange infrastructure.

‚ö†Ô∏è Why It Might Not (The Risks)

1. Monthly Unlock Pressure

~9.9M HYPE tokens unlock every month through late 2027 (Tokenomist data). At current prices, that's ~$292M in new supply hitting the market monthly. The Feb 6 unlock of $340M was absorbed well, but 18+ more months of this is a constant headwind.

2. Perp Volume ‚â† Platform Value

Coinbase has custody services, staking infrastructure, a regulatory moat, and revenue diversification. Hyperliquid is (currently) a perps exchange with a developing EVM. Volume dominance doesn't automatically translate to ecosystem dominance.

3. HyperEVM Is Unproven

The Coinbase market cap flip requires HyperEVM to attract real TVL, DeFi protocols, and developers beyond the trading use case. That hasn't happened yet. A chain built for perps needs to prove it can be a general-purpose L1.

4. Solana and BNB Are Different Beasts

Solana ($47.6B) and BNB ($85.2B) are general-purpose ecosystems with massive developer communities, DeFi TVL, NFTs, consumer apps, and years of network effects. Flipping them means Hyperliquid needs to become an entire ecosystem, not just a best-in-class exchange. That's a fundamentally different challenge.

üéØ My Verdict

COINBASE FLIP: PROBABLE

Already done by volume. By market cap, HYPE's FDV ($28.3B) is already within 1.7x of COIN (~$47B). If buybacks continue absorbing unlock pressure and HyperEVM gains traction, a circulating market cap flip is realistic within 2026. The flywheel economics are strong, and Coinbase is on the wrong side of the centralized-vs-decentralized trend.

SOLANA FLIP: POSSIBLE BUT HARDER

Similar market cap range ($47.6B), but Solana is a general-purpose L1 with a massive ecosystem. HYPE flipping SOL by market cap requires becoming more than a trading platform. HIP-3 and HIP-4 are steps in the right direction ‚Äî but Solana has years of developer ecosystem, DeFi composability, and consumer apps that Hyperliquid hasn't built yet.

BNB FLIP: UNLIKELY SHORT-TERM

At $85.2B, BNB requires a 12x from current circulating market cap. BNB also benefits from Binance's centralized exchange dominance, BSC ecosystem, and a different tokenomics model. This is a 2027+ scenario that depends on Hyperliquid executing on everything and BNB not growing. Possible in a blow-off top, but not a base case.

How I Formed This Opinion

I pulled live market data from CoinGecko and CoinPaprika to get accurate prices and market caps. I then calculated the exact multiples needed for each "flip" scenario. For the qualitative analysis, I read the primary Artemis data, five independent news sources covering the volume milestone, and four sources on recent protocol upgrades (HIP-3, HIP-4, Coinbase listing, tokenomics changes).

My opinion weighs the quantitative gap (how many x's needed) against the qualitative catalysts (flywheel economics, product expansion, market trends). The Coinbase flip is the most realistic because: (a) the FDV gap is small, (b) volume already dominates, (c) Coinbase stock is weakening while HYPE strengthens, and (d) the fee buyback mechanism creates compounding buy pressure. The Solana/BNB flips require Hyperliquid to transcend its current identity as a trading platform ‚Äî possible, but a fundamentally bigger ask.
